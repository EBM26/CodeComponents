The Uncomfortable Prime Emotions
Emotion Triggering Thoughts Adaptive Purpose Unhealthy Response Healthy Response
Fear Threats and dangers Protect the self Avoiding the fear Facing the fear
Sadness Loss Teaches meaning of that which was lost Depression or avoidance of the sadness Mourn the loss then let go and move on with life
Anger Rules of self broken by others. They "should" act in a certain way Expressing our dislike. "I want it to be different than it is" Aggression* or avoidance of the anger Assertiveness**
Guilt Rules of self broken by self. I "should" have acted in a certain way Moral compass of the self Self blame or avoidance of the guilt Take responsibility / learn from mistakes / self-forgiveness
Shame Pointing out flaws of the self. I "should" be better Teach us self-love and acceptance Self-loathing / secrecy / judgment of others Self-love / self-acceptance / self-compassion

*Aggressiveness - external control. Trying to force someone give us what we want.

**Assertiveness - internal control. Controlling on the emotions and actions of the self.

The "should" mentality is what makes people very rigid, controlling, and full of fear. Learn to be flexible with life's situations, outcomes, and others' behaviors and simply "mourn" the loss of outcomes that are different that what you expected.

The root of all these emotions is fear. We are afraid of the loss that might occur to us that we can't let go of. In the root of fear lies shame; where judgment of self and others comes from. Learn to accept, love, and have compassion towards yourself and others.
