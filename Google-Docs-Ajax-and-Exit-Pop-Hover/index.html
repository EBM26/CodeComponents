    What is glycation?

    Your body converts many of the things you eat every day into sugar (glucose). Over time, these sugar molecules build up and start latching onto your skin’s protein molecules — this process is called glycation. And glycation causes harmful mutant compounds, called glycation end products (AGEs) to form. Not only do AGEs weaken your skin’s structural proteins (causing it to loosen and sag), they also increase inflammation — which allows signs of aging like wrinkles, discoloration, loss of radiance, and many others to form faster and faster on your skin’s surface.

    Glycation affects everyone, and visible glycation damage typically starts showing up on a person’s skin around age 35 (which is why it’s often mistaken for ‘natural aging’). Once the unsightly effects of glycation set in, they can accumulate at a rapid rate — adding years to your formally-youthful appearance.
